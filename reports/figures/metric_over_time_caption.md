Validation accuracy improved modestly from the majority baseline to structured speech features and peaked with the locked interaction-logistic model. The highlighted-text semantic ablation weakened performance, and accuracy collapsed under the closed Northwestern+Gonzaga cross-tournament setting, suggesting strong distribution shift. Pair-level modeling also performed poorly, while the retrospective Shirley/team-strength model was much stronger, supporting the conclusion that latent team strength explains more outcome variation than parser-derived speech structure alone.
